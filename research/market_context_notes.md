# Market Context Research Notes (For Simulation Enrichment)

## Pest Control Industry
- ~32,000-34,000 companies in the US (2024-2025)
- Industry revenue: $17.4B-$26.1B depending on source/year
- Average annual revenue per company: ~$401,900
- Residential retention benchmark: 70-90%, target 82%+
- Commercial retention benchmark: 94%+
- A 500-customer book loses 100-150 customers/year at industry average
- Top CRM systems: FieldRoutes (40 of PCT Top 100), PestPak (dominant among larger/older companies, especially East Coast), GorillaDesk (smaller companies)
- FieldRoutes customers grow 29.55% faster than non-FieldRoutes customers in PCT Top 100

## Lawn Care Industry
- US market: $57.77B (2024), projected $89.47B by 2033
- Subscription contracts hold 66.45% of market (2025)
- Highly subscription-based, very similar to pest control model
- Strong parallel to pest control in terms of recurring revenue, seasonal patterns, churn dynamics

## HVAC/Plumbing
- US home services market: >$600B total
- Moving toward subscription/membership models but historically transactional
- ~20% annual churn on maintenance plan customers
- ServiceTitan dominant CRM for larger operations
- Housecall Pro for small-mid sized
- Jobber also significant player
- Less subscription-native than pest control or lawn care

## Cleaning Services
- Global market: ~$415.93B (2024), projected $616.98B by 2030
- Mix of residential and commercial
- Residential cleaning more subscription-oriented
- Commercial cleaning typically contract-based (annual)
- Technology adoption lower than pest control

## CRM/Software Landscape by Vertical
- Pest Control: FieldRoutes, PestPak, GorillaDesk, Briostack
- Lawn Care: ServiceTitan, Jobber, Service Autopilot, Real Green
- HVAC/Plumbing: ServiceTitan, Housecall Pro, Jobber, FieldEdge
- Cleaning: Jobber, Housecall Pro, ZenMaid, Launch27
- Key insight: CRM fragmentation across verticals means RevHawk integration strategy must be vertical-specific
