# Post-Simulation Chat Summary: David Park

**Persona:** David Park — Director of Partnerships at SaaS company
**Original Simulation:** focus_group
**Disposition:** skeptical (Skepticism: 6/10)
**Follow-up Exchanges:** 3

## Conversation

**You (Turn 1):** Hey David Park, thanks for the candid feedback in the focus group. You seemed hesitant about the Slack approach. What would it actually take to get you to try this?

**David Park:** Thanks for reaching out. To be honest, the "Slack approach" felt like another tool to integrate, which is exactly what I'm trying to avoid. We're already juggling Impact.com, Salesforce, and our custom internal tools. Adding another platform, even one as ubiquitous as Slack, just feels like more context switching and a potential data silo.

What it would take for me to try this is a very clear, demonstrable integration with our existing tech stack, specifically Impact.com and Salesforce. I need to see how this doesn't just add another step, but genuinely streamlines our current, fragmented workflow and provides a unified view without requiring my limited team to become IT support for another new system. And honestly, I need to see how it clearly justifies the spend by proving ROI, which is always a tough sell for the affiliate channel.

**You (Turn 2):** What if we offered a 30-day free pilot where we connect to your Impact.com data in a sandboxed environment — no Slack integration needed initially, just a daily email digest of insights?

**David Park:** A 30-day free pilot with *no* Slack integration and a daily email digest definitely piques my interest. The sandboxed environment is key for us given our IT policies, and starting with a digest reduces the immediate burden on my team for learning a new interface.

However, the real value for me still comes back to how this pilot would explicitly prove ROI and partner quality. Can this digest show me things I'm not already getting from Impact.com's reporting, and how would it help me identify top-performing partners or areas for optimization that I'm currently missing? That's what I need to justify dedicating any team bandwidth to even a free trial.

**You (Turn 3):** If the pilot showed you were missing $50K+ in revenue from dormant affiliates, would that be enough to justify going through the vendor approval process?

**David Park:** That's a very specific and compelling scenario. If your pilot could demonstrably show us we're missing $50K+ in *attributable* revenue from dormant affiliates – meaning it's revenue we could realistically recover or generate – that would absolutely get my attention and make the vendor approval process worthwhile.

The key here is "demonstrably" and "attributable." I've been burned by vendors promising big numbers that don't materialize or are difficult to prove out in our specific environment. So, the pilot would need to clearly lay out the methodology for identifying that missed revenue and the actionable steps we could take to capture it, beyond just highlighting a number.
